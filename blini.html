Blini

Blini is a traditional Russian recipe! I spent a semester abroad in Russia and my host mother would make me blini for dessert...and sometimes breakfast! It was my favorite meal and I strongly encourage everyone to make it. I found the original recipe here.

The ingredients you'll need are:

  • 2 eggs
  • 2 cups of milk at room temperature (not hot, not cold)
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1/3 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tbsp veggie oil
  • 2 tbsp melted butter
  • 1 cup flour
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

To make:

  1. Whisk together the eggs and then add milk. Whisk until blended.
  2. Add sugar, salt, baking soda, veggie oil and butter. Mix all ingredients.
  3. Gradually add the flour and mix well until no lumps are visible.
  4. Heat a medium non-stick pan over medium heat. Spray with Pam.
  5. Add about a 1/4 cup of the batter to the pan.
  6. let cook for about 40 seconds on each side (this is what we found worked for us).
We found that the best way to flip it was to do it quickly. Try to make sure that the top is not too liquid-y before flipping -- this is why we left it longer than the recommend 30 seconds. Try to make as clean of a flip as possible but don't feel discouraged if it takes some practice! We had quite a few ugly blini before some pretty ones.
We served our blini with jam. I spread the jam on it when it was flat and then folded it into triangles. See imagine below! When I was in Russia, my host mother would make me blini with chocolate. We haven't tried that yet, but we plan to!
